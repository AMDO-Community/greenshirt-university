201 INTRODUCTION TO AUTOMATED LOGISTICS ENVIRONMENT

201.1 SYSTEM COMPONENTS AND COMPONENT PARTS​

Identify the following system components and component parts and discuss the designated items for each: A. What is its function?
B. Where is it located?

201.1.2 NALCOMIS OOMA [A,B]​

A. Function

  • Provides the automated organizational level maintenance management system to document, track, and report O-level maintenance actions, aircraft status, inspections, discrepancies, component removals/installs, ADB entries, and maintenance performance data.

B. Location

  • Resides on the activity’s networked NALCOMIS server environment, accessed from authorized client workstations at the organizational maintenance activity (squadron, det, or site).

201.1.3 Server / Det Server [A,B]​

A. Function

  • Hosts the NALCOMIS application and databases, processes transactions, stores maintenance data, supports report generation, user authentication, backups, and connectivity between clients and the database.

B. Location

  • Physically located in the command’s designated secure communications/server space (or approved IT/server room).
  • A det server is located at the det site when required to support detached operations and local system availability.

201.1.4 OOMA maintenance subsystems [A,B]​

A. Function

  • Provide the functional modules used to manage and document O-level maintenance, including aircraft status, discrepancy documentation, inspections, component tracking, work orders/MAFs, and reporting.

B. Location

  • Within the OOMA application accessed on approved client terminals connected to the NALCOMIS server/database.

201.1.4 Management Reports and their uses [A,B]​

A. Function / Use
a. Aircraft daily status

  • Displays aircraft status by BUNO, including MC/NMC conditions and key drivers for daily readiness management.

b. Work center workload

  • Shows workload by work center (open/assigned tasks), used to balance manpower, prioritize work, and manage production.

c. Aircraft/Equipment workload

  • Shows workload by specific aircraft or equipment, used to plan downtime, schedule maintenance, and forecast workload.

d. Aircraft material status

  • Shows parts/material status affecting maintenance (AWP/NMCS related visibility), used to manage constraints and expediting.

e. Inspections by assembly code

  • Lists inspections grouped by assembly code, used to plan and verify inspection compliance by system/assembly.

f. Scheduled inspections

  • Shows upcoming scheduled inspections, used for short-, mid-, and long-range maintenance planning.

g. Work order audit trail

  • Shows transaction history for work orders/MAFs, used for accountability, troubleshooting data issues, and audit support.

h. Maintenance 1 (Consolidated Performance Metrics)

  • Consolidated maintenance metrics, used by leadership to assess overall maintenance performance.

i. Maintenance 2 (Aircraft readiness degradation and utilization summary)

  • Summarizes readiness impacts and utilization, used to identify drivers degrading readiness and operational tempo effects.

j. Maintenance 3 (Sub-system capability impact by work unit code)

  • Shows subsystem capability impacts by WUC, used to identify which systems most affect mission capability.

k. Maintenance 4 (Detailed mission and maintenance data by aircraft system)

  • Detailed mission and maintenance by system, used for deeper analysis and reliability/trend evaluation.

l. Maintenance 5 (Maintenance man hours)

  • Tracks man-hours by categories/work centers, used for manpower planning and productivity analysis.

m. Maintenance 6 (Detailed data extract)

  • Data extract for detailed analysis, used for advanced trend reviews, briefs, and data calls.

B. Location

  • Generated within OOMA reporting functions and viewed/printed from authorized terminals with report access.

201.1.5 Automated Aircraft Discrepancy Book (ADB) [A,B]​

A. Function

  • Provides the electronic ADB to record, review, and manage aircraft discrepancies, maintenance actions, inspections due, limits, and history.

B. Location

  • Within OOMA Automated ADB module accessed from authorized OOMA terminals.

ADB sections
a. A Sheet

  • Primary discrepancy listing and documentation page for aircraft discrepancies.

b. Daily maintenance record

  • Displays daily maintenance actions/entries supporting daily aircraft management.

c. Turnaround maintenance record

  • Displays maintenance actions tied to turnaround servicing between flights.

d. Aircraft limitations

  • Displays aircraft operating limitations/restrictions affecting flight/maintenance decisions.

e. Daily/Turnaround-local card

  • Local card view for quick daily/turnaround tracking as configured.

f. Last 10 A Sheets

  • Shows the most recent 10 A Sheets for quick review of recent discrepancy history.

g. Inspections near due

  • Lists inspections approaching due, used for planning and compliance.

h. Removals near due

  • Lists components approaching removal limits, used for planning and readiness.

i. Summary

  • High-level overview of aircraft status, key discrepancies, and maintenance drivers.

j. Engine/APU/Prop data

  • Displays powerplant related data (as applicable), used for monitoring and record accuracy.

k. Open work orders

  • Lists open work orders/MAFs affecting the aircraft, used to manage production.

l. Awaiting Maintenance Control Approval

  • Shows items pending MC action, used to ensure proper control and status closure.

m. Closed work orders last 10 flights

  • Shows recently closed work orders for recent flight windows, used for review and trend awareness.

201.1.6 Technical directive procedures [A,B]​

a. Push down procedures
A. Function

  • Establishes how TD requirements are applied/assigned within OOMA so the correct aircraft/equipment and affected records are identified and tracked.

B. Location

  • Within OOMA TD management functions accessible to authorized users.

b. Issue and sign-off
A. Function

  • Establishes how TD compliance actions are issued to maintenance and how completion/verification is documented and closed in OOMA.

B. Location

  • Within OOMA TD action documentation and sign-off workflow.

201.1.7 WorkCenter [A,B]​

a. DIFM
A. Function

  • Tracks repairable components designated as Due-In From Maintenance, supporting accountability and repairable flow.

B. Location

  • Work center / supply interface views within OOMA used to monitor DIFM status.

b. Work center workload
A. Function

  • Displays tasks/MAFs assigned to the work center to manage production, scheduling, and priorities.

B. Location

  • Work center workload views within OOMA.

c. AWP
A. Function

  • Tracks jobs delayed due to Awaiting Parts and associated requisition status.

B. Location

  • Work center / maintenance control AWP tracking views and reports within OOMA.

d. N codes – status changes and reviewing
A. Function

  • Status codes used to reflect maintenance status changes and track workflow progression in the system.

B. Location

  • Seen in work order/MAF status fields and review screens within OOMA.

201.1.8 Explain the purpose of a Maintenance Action Form (MAF) / Work Order (W/O) as applied to OOMA documentation [A,B]​

  • The MAF/W/O is the official maintenance documentation record used to capture the discrepancy, troubleshooting, corrective action, parts/material usage, man-hours, and required inspections/sign-offs.
  • It provides traceability, accountability, maintenance history, and supports reporting, readiness metrics, and compliance requirements.

201.2 PRINCIPLES OF OPERATION​

201.2.1 What functional requirements of the NAMP are satisfied by the organizational maintenance activity’s OOMA?​

OOMA satisfies NAMP functional requirements by enabling the command to:

  • Document and control O-level maintenance actions using standardized electronic maintenance records.
  • Track aircraft status (MC/NMC) and readiness drivers for operational decision making.
  • Record discrepancies, corrective actions, inspections, and component history to support airworthiness and compliance.
  • Support QA processes through controlled documentation, inspection sign-offs, and auditability.
  • Provide maintenance management reports and data to support production control, trend analysis, and readiness reporting.
  • Maintain an auditable record of maintenance, including TD compliance tracking and configuration management elements required at the organizational level.

201.2.2 Using a diagram of this system, state the path of the following​

(Provided as step-by-step flow in lieu of a drawn diagram)

a. O-Level MAF flow from initiation to sign off for scheduled maintenance​

  1. Phase (look/fix)
  • Scheduled event identified and initiated in OOMA.
  • Work order/MAF opened and tasks generated/assigned to work centers.
  • Maintenance performed and documented (actions, parts, man-hours).
  • Required inspections performed and documented by qualified inspectors.
  • Maintenance Control reviews for completeness/accuracy.
  • MAF closed and aircraft records updated.
  1. Planned maintenance
  • Planned task identified and initiated in OOMA.
  • MAF opened, tasks assigned to appropriate work center(s).
  • Maintenance performed and documented.
  • Required inspection/verification completed and recorded.
  • Maintenance Control verifies documentation and closes MAF.
  1. Special inspection
  • Special inspection requirement identified and initiated in OOMA.
  • MAF created and work assigned.
  • Inspection accomplished and documented.
  • Discrepancies corrected as required and documented.
  • QA/inspector sign-offs completed as required.
  • Maintenance Control reviews and closes MAF.

b. Unscheduled maintenance​

  1. Conditional inspection
  • Conditional requirement identified (event-driven trigger).
  • MAF initiated and assigned to work center(s).
  • Inspection performed and documented.
  • Any required corrective actions documented.
  • Required inspection sign-offs completed.
  • Maintenance Control reviews and closes MAF.
  1. Repair
  • Discrepancy documented and MAF initiated.
  • Work assigned to appropriate work center.
  • Troubleshooting and repair performed and documented.
  • Parts/material usage recorded.
  • Required inspection sign-offs completed.
  • Maintenance Control reviews and closes MAF.

c. Work request​

  • Work request identified and entered in OOMA.
  • Work request routed/assigned to appropriate maintenance activity/work center.
  • Work accomplished and documented.
  • Any inspections/sign-offs completed as required.
  • Maintenance Control final review and closure completed.

d. Cannibalization​

  • Cannibalization requirement identified and authorized.
  • Donor and receiver aircraft/equipment identified in OOMA.
  • Cannibalization MAF/work order initiated and documented.
  • Removal/installation actions documented with part/serial tracking as required.
  • Required inspections completed and signed off.
  • Maintenance Control review and closure, aircraft records updated for both donor and receiver.

e. Facilitate Other Maintenance (FOM)​

  • FOM requirement identified and initiated in OOMA.
  • Work documented to reflect support provided to other maintenance actions.
  • Work center actions recorded (man-hours, support actions, any parts usage).
  • Required sign-offs completed.
  • Maintenance Control reviews and closes as appropriate.

f. TD​

  • TD requirement identified and applied to applicable aircraft/equipment.
  • TD action initiated in OOMA with work assignment.
  • Compliance actions performed and documented.
  • Verification/inspection sign-offs completed as required.
  • Maintenance Control closes action and updates compliance status.

g. Assist​

  • Assist requirement identified and initiated.
  • Work documented indicating assisting work center support provided.
  • Actions, man-hours, and any material usage recorded.
  • Required sign-offs completed.
  • Maintenance Control reviews and closes.

h. I-Level MAF flow from initiation to sign off for:​

  1. AMSU induction of a WRA
  • WRA identified for induction to I-level/AMSU.
  • Induction documentation initiated.
  • WRA inducted, screened, and processed.
  • Repair actions documented and tracked.
  • Final inspection/QA as required.
  • Sign-off and closure completed, status updated for return.
  1. Suffix MAF generation of an SRA
  • WRA MAF initiated for repair action.
  • During repair, additional SRA actions identified.
  • Suffix MAF generated to document SRA-level work.
  • SRA work performed and documented.
  • Required inspections/sign-offs completed.
  • Suffix MAF closed, parent WRA MAF updated and closed upon completion.
  1. Inter IMA repair and return
  • Requirement identified for inter-IMA support.
  • Documentation initiated and item routed to supporting IMA.
  • Repair performed at supporting IMA and documented.
  • Item returned and receipt documented.
  • Final sign-offs completed and closure updated.
  1. D-Action
  • Depot level action identified and initiated in documentation flow.
  • Item prepared, documented, and routed for depot processing.
  • Status tracked through repair pipeline.
  • Receipt/completion updates recorded.
  • Closure completed when action resolved and records updated.
  1. TD
  • TD requirement applied to I-level process as applicable.
  • Action initiated, work performed and documented.
  • QA/inspection sign-offs completed as required.
  • TD compliance recorded and closed.
  1. Work request
  • Work request initiated and routed within I-level system flow.
  • Work accomplished and documented.
  • Required inspections performed.
  • Closure completed with status updates.
  1. PM
  • Preventive maintenance requirement identified.
  • PM action initiated, performed, and documented.
  • Inspections/sign-offs completed as required.
  • Closure completed and records updated.
  1. Test and check
  • Test/check requirement initiated.
  • Test procedures executed and results documented.
  • Out-of-tolerance discrepancies corrected and documented as needed.
  • Final verification and sign-off completed.
  • Closure recorded.
  1. Cannibalization
  • Cannibalization requirement identified and authorized at I-level.
  • Donor and receiver identified and documented.
  • Removal/installation actions documented, including tracking.
  • Inspections/sign-offs completed.
  • Closure recorded and records updated.

201.3 PARAMETERS / OPERATING LIMITS​

201.3.1 Discuss the security considerations that apply to an individual’s login and password [A,B]​

A. Allowable operating limits

  • Login credentials must be used only by the assigned individual.
  • Passwords must meet required complexity and change requirements per command policy.
  • Passwords must not be shared, written in unsecured locations, or reused improperly.

B. Where parameters are monitored

  • System access is monitored via system security controls, audit logs, and administrative user management functions.

C. When required

  • At all times when accessing NALCOMIS/OOMA and any maintenance information systems.
  • During account creation, routine access, password changes, and security events.

201.3.2 Discuss the QA involvement for completion of the following MAFs [A,C]​

a. Routine maintenance
A. Allowable operating limits

  • QA involvement required when designated by policy for specific tasks, inspections, or critical steps.

C. When required

  • When the MAF requires QA verification, CDI/CDQAR/QAR sign-off, or audit requirements based on task type and local procedures.

b. Safety of flight
A. Allowable operating limits

  • QA involvement is mandatory for safety of flight related maintenance requiring QA-level verification/inspection and proper certification.

C. When required

  • Whenever the MAF involves safety of flight discrepancies, critical systems, or designated QA sign-off requirements.

c. Inspection
A. Allowable operating limits

  • QA involvement required for inspections where QA-level verification is specified, including critical inspections and compliance verification.

C. When required

  • When inspection tasks require CDI/CDQAR/QAR involvement, verification of completion, and compliance sign-off.

201.4 SYSTEM INTERFACE​

201.4.1 Discuss how OOMA and OIMA interface and interact​

  • OOMA documents and manages organizational level maintenance actions and aircraft status.
  • OIMA documents intermediate level maintenance actions, component repairs, inductions, and I-level workflows.
  • The interface enables coordinated tracking of repairables and maintenance actions across O- and I-levels, ensuring:
    • proper component history and status visibility,
    • accurate repairable pipeline tracking,
    • consistent maintenance documentation,
    • and readiness reporting alignment between activities.
