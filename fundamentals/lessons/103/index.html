103 Manpower / Personnel Management Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Manpower management ensures:

  • Right Sailor
  • Right skill/NEC
  • Right place
  • Right time

The goal is to maximize readiness by aligning billets, qualifications, and training with mission requirements while minimizing gaps and excess.

103.1 Discuss the purpose and search options in Billet Based Distribution (BBD)​

Billet Based Distribution assigns Sailors to specific billets instead of generic bodies.

Purpose:

  • Match Sailor skills/NEGs/NECs to exact billet requirements
  • Improve readiness and fit
  • Reduce mismatches and gapped billets

Search options include:

  • Rate/NEC
  • Location
  • Sea/Shore
  • Platform
  • PRD window
  • Special programs

103.2 Identify key components of BBD​

a. PACT Sailor​

Professional Apprenticeship Career Track Sailor without a permanent rating, assigned for experience prior to striking.

b. Alignment​

Matching Sailor qualifications and NECs to billet requirements.

c. BA (Billet Authorized)​

Number of billets officially approved for a command.

d. Bridged NEC​

Temporary substitution where a related NEC may fill a billet.

e. BSC (Billet Sequence Code)​

Unique code identifying each specific billet.

f. C-Way​

Career Waypoints. Retention and reenlistment approval system.

g. DNEC​

Distribution NEC used for detailing and assignment purposes.

h. EDLN​

Estimated Date Loss to the Navy, projected separation/transfer.

i. FAC​

Functional Area Code identifying type of billet or mission.

j. Fill​

Billet currently occupied.

k. Floating NEC​

NEC not tied to a specific billet, provides flexibility.

l. Lock Status​

Indicates whether a Sailor is protected from orders due to PRD or assignment rules.

m. MRT (Manning Requirement Type)​

Identifies type of manpower requirement such as sea, shore, or training.

n. Fit​

How well a Sailor matches billet requirements.

o. PNEC / SNEC​

Primary and Secondary NECs held by a Sailor.

p. PG / TG​

Pay Grade / Target Grade of billet.

q. QOA​

Quality of Alignment. Metric measuring how well billets are properly filled.

r. Unaligned Personnel​

Sailors not properly matched to billets.

s. Unfunded Billet​

Authorized billet without funding.

t. Vacancies​

Unfilled billets that create readiness gaps.

103.3 Discuss the purpose and components of the Activity Manning Document (AMD)​

The AMD is the command’s official manpower blueprint.

Purpose: Defines how many people and what skills are required to accomplish the mission.

Components:

  • Billets
  • Paygrades
  • Rates
  • NECs
  • Work centers
  • Funding

Think of it as the "organizational design document" for the command.

103.4 Manpower requirement information in the AMD​

Requirements show:

  • What skills are needed
  • How many personnel required
  • Workload based justification
  • Mission driven needs

These represent what the command SHOULD have.

103.5 Manpower authorization information in the AMD​

Authorizations show:

  • Funded billets
  • What Navy will actually provide

These represent what the command IS allowed to fill.

Difference: Requirements = ideal
Authorizations = reality

103.6 Purpose of the Enlisted Manning Inquiry Report (EMIR)​

EMIR provides a snapshot of:

  • On board personnel
  • Gaps
  • Gains/losses
  • NEC mismatches

Used by leadership to:

  • Track readiness
  • Plan cross leveling
  • Prioritize detailing requests

It is a readiness decision tool.

103.7 Discuss the purpose of the Enlisted Distribution and Verification Process (EDVP)​

EDVP validates:

  • Sailors
  • Billets
  • NECs
  • Fit and alignment

Ensures:

  • Accurate manning data
  • Proper distribution
  • Correct skill placement

It helps TYCOMs and detailers fix mismatches before they affect readiness.

103.8 Discuss roles of manpower stakeholders​

Command​

Identifies requirements and reports gaps.

TYCOM​

Manages fleet level manning priorities.

Detailers​

Assign Sailors to billets.

NAVMAC​

Validates manpower requirements analytically.

Sailors​

Maintain qualifications and communicate career plans.

103.9 Discuss Fleet Training Management and Planning System (FLTMPS)​

FLTMPS tracks:

  • Training
  • Qualifications
  • Schools
  • Certifications

Purpose:

  • Identify training gaps
  • Schedule schools
  • Maintain readiness
  • Support inspections and boards

It is the authoritative training record.

103.10 Discuss Navy Training Management Planning System (NTMPS)​

NTMPS integrates:

  • Manpower
  • Training
  • Readiness

Helps commands forecast:

  • Required skills
  • Training pipelines
  • Qualification timelines

Supports long term readiness planning.

103.11 Discuss Billet Based Distribution benefits to readiness​

Benefits:

  • Right skills in right billets
  • Fewer NEC mismatches
  • Faster qualification
  • Reduced training waste
  • Improved mission capability

Bottom line: Better alignment = better aircraft readiness.

103.12 Discuss detailing considerations​

Detailers consider:

  • NEC fit
  • Sea/Shore flow
  • Career timing
  • Advancement
  • Command priority
  • Special programs

Goal is balance between Navy needs and Sailor career progression.

103.13 Discuss the impact of manning on maintenance readiness​

Good manning:

  • Faster turnaround
  • Fewer backlogs
  • Better QA
  • Safer operations

Poor manning:

  • Burnout
  • Delays
  • Increased errors
  • Mishaps

Maintenance effectiveness is directly tied to manpower quality and quantity.

103.14 Discuss cross leveling​

Cross leveling is temporarily shifting personnel between units to fill critical gaps.

Used when:

  • Deployments
  • Surge ops
  • Shortfalls

Provides short term readiness but is not a permanent fix.

103.15 Discuss Sailor lifecycle management​

Includes:

  • Recruiting
  • Training
  • Assignment
  • Retention
  • Advancement
  • Separation

Proper lifecycle management ensures long term force sustainability.

103.16 Discuss PRD (Projected Rotation Date)​

PRD:

  • Expected transfer date
  • Drives detailing window
  • Impacts turnover and continuity

Commands plan training and qualifications around PRDs.

103.17 Discuss how local directives/SOPs support manpower management​

Local policies:

  • Define watch bills
  • Set qualification standards
  • Assign duties
  • Establish training requirements

They translate manpower into executable readiness.

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "Why does manpower management matter to a maintenance leader?"

Answer:

"Because aircraft readiness is directly tied to having the right skill mix. Even the best maintenance program fails if we don’t have properly trained Sailors in the right billets. My job is to align manpower, training, and qualifications to ensure safe and efficient maintenance operations."
