122 Aviation Maintenance Risk Management, Human Factors, and Error Reduction Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Most maintenance mishaps are not caused by lack of knowledge.

They are caused by:

  • Fatigue
  • Complacency
  • Poor communication
  • Time pressure
  • Distraction
  • Weak supervision

Risk management reduces human error.

As a maintenance leader, you must manage people and risk, not just equipment.

Safe decisions produce ready aircraft.

122.1 Discuss the purpose of risk management in maintenance​

Risk management:

  • Identifies hazards
  • Assesses risk
  • Implements controls
  • Prevents mishaps

Purpose: Reduce probability and severity of accidents while accomplishing the mission.

122.2 Discuss the relationship between risk and readiness​

Uncontrolled risk:

  • Causes mishaps
  • Damages aircraft
  • Injures personnel
  • Reduces availability

Controlled risk:

  • Preserves assets
  • Maintains mission capability

Safety directly supports readiness.

122.3 Discuss Operational Risk Management (ORM)​

ORM is a structured decision-making process used to:

  • Identify hazards
  • Evaluate risk
  • Apply controls

Used in all maintenance and operational activities.

ORM must be continuous and deliberate.

122.4 State the five steps of ORM​

  1. Identify hazards
  2. Assess hazards
  3. Make risk decisions
  4. Implement controls
  5. Supervise

These steps apply at all levels.

122.5 Discuss hazard identification methods​

Methods include:

  • Walkthroughs
  • Inspections
  • Safety reports
  • Trend analysis
  • Experience

Early identification reduces mishaps.

Every Sailor is responsible for reporting hazards.

122.6 Discuss risk assessment​

Risk assessment evaluates:

  • Probability of occurrence
  • Severity of consequence

Determines overall risk level.

Used to prioritize controls.

122.7 Discuss risk control measures​

Controls include:

  • Engineering controls
  • Administrative controls
  • PPE
  • Supervision
  • Training

Goal: Reduce risk to acceptable levels.

Multiple layers provide better protection.

122.8 Discuss supervision in risk management​

Supervision ensures:

  • Procedures are followed
  • Controls are effective
  • Unsafe acts are corrected

Active leadership presence reduces errors.

Supervision is continuous.

122.9 Discuss human factors in maintenance​

Human factors include:

  • Fatigue
  • Stress
  • Complacency
  • Inexperience
  • Poor communication
  • Distraction

These increase likelihood of mistakes.

Understanding human factors prevents errors.

122.10 Discuss fatigue effects on performance​

Fatigue causes:

  • Slower reaction time
  • Poor judgment
  • Reduced attention
  • Increased mistakes

Fatigued personnel are unsafe.

Fatigue must be managed deliberately.

122.11 Discuss fatigue mitigation strategies​

Includes:

  • Adequate rest
  • Shift planning
  • Workload control
  • Crew rotation
  • Supervisory awareness

Purpose: Maintain alert and effective personnel.

122.12 Discuss complacency​

Complacency occurs when:

  • Tasks feel routine
  • Risks are underestimated
  • Procedures are skipped

Leads to preventable errors.

Maintain vigilance and standards.

122.13 Discuss communication errors​

Poor communication leads to:

  • Misunderstood tasks
  • Missed steps
  • Incorrect installations

Use:

  • Clear instructions
  • Read-backs
  • Standard terminology

Effective communication prevents mistakes.

122.14 Discuss distractions and task interruption​

Distractions:

  • Break concentration
  • Increase error probability

Mitigation:

  • Minimize interruptions
  • Resume using checklists
  • Re-verify work

Focus improves quality.

122.15 Discuss stress management​

Stress affects:

  • Judgment
  • Performance
  • Morale

Leaders must:

  • Monitor workload
  • Provide support
  • Encourage reporting

Managing stress improves safety and readiness.

122.16 Discuss time pressure risks​

Time pressure:

  • Encourages shortcuts
  • Reduces attention to detail
  • Increases mistakes

Mission urgency must not override safety.

Slow is smooth. Smooth is fast.

122.17 Discuss checklist usage​

Checklists:

  • Standardize procedures
  • Reduce memory reliance
  • Prevent missed steps

Must be used consistently.

Do not rely on memory alone.

122.18 Discuss procedural compliance​

Maintenance must follow:

  • Technical manuals
  • Instructions
  • Safety requirements

Skipping steps increases risk.

Procedures exist because someone was hurt before.

122.19 Discuss reporting of hazards and near misses​

Reporting:

  • Identifies hidden risks
  • Prevents future mishaps
  • Improves processes

Non-punitive reporting encourages participation.

Lessons learned protect the fleet.

122.20 Discuss maintenance error investigations​

Investigations determine:

  • Root causes
  • Contributing factors
  • Corrective actions

Purpose: Prevent recurrence.

Focus on system improvement, not blame.

122.21 Discuss trend analysis for error reduction​

Analyzes:

  • Repeat discrepancies
  • QA findings
  • Mishap data

Purpose: Identify systemic weaknesses.

Data driven improvements reduce errors.

122.22 Discuss safety culture​

Safety culture exists when:

  • Personnel follow procedures
  • Hazards are reported
  • Leaders enforce standards
  • Everyone values safety

Strong culture reduces mishaps.

Culture starts with leadership.

122.23 Discuss personal responsibility for safety​

Each individual must:

  • Follow procedures
  • Use PPE
  • Report hazards
  • Stop unsafe acts

Safety is everyone’s responsibility.

Not just leadership.

122.24 Discuss the relationship between human performance and maintenance quality​

Better human performance:

  • Fewer errors
  • Higher quality work
  • Less rework
  • Improved readiness

Managing people effectively improves outcomes.

Human performance drives maintenance quality.

122.25 Discuss the responsibilities of an Aviation Maintenance Officer for risk management and human factors​

The AMDO must:

  • Enforce ORM
  • Manage fatigue and workload
  • Supervise operations
  • Promote reporting
  • Correct unsafe practices
  • Lead by example

Bottom line: Managing risk and human factors prevents mishaps and protects readiness.

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "What causes most maintenance mishaps?"

Answer:

"Human factors such as fatigue, complacency, and poor communication. Effective risk management and supervision reduce these errors and protect readiness."
