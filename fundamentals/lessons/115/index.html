115 Aviation Material Readiness and Asset Management Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Material readiness means:

  • Aircraft available
  • Components serviceable
  • Support equipment ready
  • Assets accounted for

Maintenance fixes problems.
Material management prevents them.

As an AMDO, you must control:

  • Assets
  • Components
  • Support equipment
  • Preservation
  • Accountability

Poor material control directly reduces aircraft availability.

115.1 Discuss the purpose of material readiness management​

Material readiness management ensures:

  • Aircraft and equipment are safe and serviceable
  • Assets are available when required
  • Resources are properly used
  • Downtime is minimized

Purpose: Maximize mission capable rates.

115.2 Discuss aircraft material condition status​

Aircraft condition reflects:

  • FMC
  • PMC
  • NMCM
  • NMCS
  • NMCB

Status indicates the aircraft’s ability to perform assigned missions.

Used to assess readiness and prioritize maintenance.

115.3 Discuss responsibilities for aircraft custody​

Custody includes:

  • Physical control
  • Security
  • Cleanliness
  • Documentation
  • Daily inspections

Plane captains and line divisions maintain custody.

Ensures aircraft remain safe and ready.

115.4 Discuss aircraft inventory management​

Includes:

  • Tracking assigned aircraft
  • Monitoring status
  • Recording transfers
  • Maintaining records

Purpose: Maintain accountability and readiness awareness.

115.5 Discuss component inventory management​

Tracks:

  • Installed components
  • Spare components
  • Repairables
  • Rotatable assets

Purpose: Ensure parts availability and configuration accuracy.

Prevents shortages and excess.

115.6 Discuss repairable asset control​

Repairables must be:

  • Tracked
  • Turned in promptly
  • Repaired
  • Reissued

Includes AVDLR management.

Purpose: Maintain repair pipeline and reduce AWP.

115.7 Discuss Beyond Capability of Maintenance (BCM) procedures​

BCM applies when:

  • Repair exceeds local capability

Actions:

  • Document discrepancy
  • Process turn-in
  • Ship to higher level maintenance

Ensures proper repair and accountability.

115.8 Discuss preservation requirements​

Preservation protects assets during:

  • Storage
  • Deployment
  • Inactivity
  • Transportation

Includes:

  • Covers
  • Corrosion prevention
  • Sealing
  • Environmental controls

Prevents deterioration and damage.

115.9 Discuss storage procedures​

Proper storage requires:

  • Environmental protection
  • Secure locations
  • Segregation
  • Proper labeling

Purpose: Maintain serviceability and accountability.

115.10 Discuss corrosion prevention and material condition​

Corrosion:

  • Weakens structure
  • Causes failures
  • Increases maintenance

Prevention includes:

  • Cleaning
  • Inspection
  • Protective coatings
  • Timely treatment

Directly impacts lifecycle and readiness.

115.11 Discuss support equipment (SE) readiness​

SE must be:

  • Operational
  • Inspected
  • Maintained
  • Calibrated
  • Available

Aircraft cannot be maintained without serviceable SE.

SE readiness equals aircraft readiness.

115.12 Discuss IMRL management​

IMRL tracks:

  • Required support equipment
  • Allowances
  • Custody
  • Maintenance status

Ensures required equipment is available and accounted for.

Supports maintenance capability.

115.13 Discuss tool and material accountability​

Includes:

  • Tool control
  • Inventory checks
  • Custody logs

Prevents:

  • FOD
  • Loss
  • Safety hazards

Accountability protects aircraft and personnel.

115.14 Discuss asset tracking systems​

Systems track:

  • Aircraft
  • Components
  • Repairables
  • SE

Provide:

  • Location
  • Status
  • History

Purpose: Real-time visibility and accountability.

115.15 Discuss aircraft transfer procedures​

Transfers require:

  • Inventory verification
  • Logbook updates
  • Configuration checks
  • Documentation completion

Ensures accountability between commands.

115.16 Discuss cannibalization impacts on material readiness​

Cannibalization:

  • Temporarily increases readiness
  • Creates donor aircraft downtime
  • Increases workload

Used only when necessary.

Indicates supply or planning deficiencies if frequent.

115.17 Discuss supply and maintenance coordination for assets​

Requires:

  • Early requisitions
  • Turn-ins
  • Accurate demand forecasting
  • Communication

Improves parts availability and reduces downtime.

115.18 Discuss material condition inspections​

Includes:

  • Periodic inspections
  • Preservation checks
  • Corrosion inspections
  • Inventory verification

Ensures equipment remains serviceable.

Prevents hidden degradation.

115.19 Discuss hazardous material storage requirements​

HAZMAT must be:

  • Labeled
  • Segregated
  • Ventilated
  • Secured

Prevents:

  • Fire
  • Health hazards
  • Environmental damage

115.20 Discuss security of aircraft and equipment​

Includes:

  • Access control
  • Locking
  • Watchstanding
  • Surveillance

Prevents theft, sabotage, and tampering.

Protects mission capability.

115.21 Discuss lifecycle management of components​

Includes:

  • Installation
  • Operation
  • Inspection
  • Repair
  • Replacement

Ensures:

  • Reliability
  • Cost control
  • Safety

Proper lifecycle management reduces failures.

115.22 Discuss readiness reporting related to material status​

Reports use:

  • Aircraft status
  • Parts availability
  • SE readiness

Purpose: Provide leadership accurate readiness picture.

Data drives decisions.

115.23 Discuss documentation requirements for assets​

Requires:

  • Logs
  • Records
  • Inventory forms
  • Maintenance history

Provides:

  • Accountability
  • Traceability
  • Legal protection

If undocumented, it does not exist.

115.24 Discuss the relationship between material readiness and mission capability​

Good material readiness:

  • Reduces downtime
  • Increases sortie generation
  • Improves reliability

Poor readiness:

  • Delays missions
  • Increases costs
  • Reduces combat effectiveness

Material condition directly affects mission success.

115.25 Discuss the responsibilities of an Aviation Maintenance Officer for material readiness​

The AMDO must:

  • Monitor aircraft status
  • Control assets
  • Manage repairables
  • Coordinate with supply
  • Enforce accountability
  • Ensure preservation and inspections

Bottom line: Material readiness determines how many aircraft are available to fight.

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "What is material readiness?"

Answer:

"It is the ability to have aircraft, components, and support equipment serviceable and available when required. Proper asset management directly increases mission capable rates and readiness."
