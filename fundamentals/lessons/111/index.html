111 Aviation Maintenance Programs Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Maintenance programs exist to:

  • Standardize procedures
  • Reduce risk
  • Prevent mishaps
  • Ensure compliance
  • Improve readiness

Programs turn policy into daily execution.

As an AMDO, you are responsible for ensuring programs are:

  • Established
  • Trained
  • Followed
  • Audited
  • Effective

If programs fail, maintenance fails.

111.1 Discuss the purpose of the Naval Aviation Maintenance Program (NAMP)​

The NAMP establishes standardized policies, procedures, and responsibilities for maintenance of naval aircraft and support equipment.

Purpose:

  • Safety
  • Standardization
  • Readiness
  • Accountability

It ensures all aviation activities perform maintenance uniformly across the fleet.

111.2 Discuss the purpose of maintenance program compliance​

Compliance ensures:

  • Procedures are followed
  • Risks are controlled
  • Inspections are passed
  • Aircraft remain airworthy

Non-compliance increases mishap risk and degrades readiness.

111.3 Discuss the Quality Assurance (QA) Program​

QA:

  • Audits maintenance practices
  • Conducts inspections
  • Investigates discrepancies
  • Tracks trends
  • Manages CDIs/CDQARs
  • Ensures NAMP compliance

Purpose: Prevent errors before they cause mishaps.

111.4 Discuss the Tool Control Program (TCP)​

Ensures all tools are:

  • Accounted for
  • Inventoried
  • Properly stored
  • Not left in aircraft

Uses:

  • Shadow boards
  • Tool logs
  • Missing tool procedures

Purpose: Prevent FOD and tool-related mishaps.

111.5 Discuss the FOD Prevention Program​

Controls foreign object damage through:

  • Walkdowns
  • Housekeeping
  • Tool accountability
  • Intake/exhaust covers
  • Immediate removal of debris

Purpose: Prevent engine and aircraft damage.

Clean deck equals safe deck.

111.6 Discuss the Hazardous Material (HAZMAT) Program​

Manages:

  • Storage
  • Handling
  • Labeling
  • Disposal
  • Training

Ensures compliance with environmental and safety regulations.

Purpose: Protect personnel and environment.

111.7 Discuss the Corrosion Prevention and Control Program (CPCP)​

Prevents and treats corrosion through:

  • Inspections
  • Cleaning
  • Protective coatings
  • Scheduled preservation

Purpose: Extend aircraft life and maintain structural integrity.

Corrosion directly impacts readiness and safety.

111.8 Discuss the Training and Qualification Program​

Tracks:

  • PQS completion
  • Certifications
  • Schools
  • Licenses

Ensures only qualified personnel perform maintenance.

If not documented, not qualified.

111.9 Discuss the Maintenance Safety Program​

Promotes safe work practices through:

  • ORM
  • PPE
  • Mishap reporting
  • Hazard identification
  • Safety training

Purpose: Reduce injuries and maintenance errors.

Safety is integrated into every evolution.

111.10 Discuss the Calibration Program​

Ensures measuring and test equipment:

  • Is accurate
  • Within tolerance
  • Calibrated on schedule
  • Properly labeled

Prevents maintenance errors caused by inaccurate instruments.

111.11 Discuss the Support Equipment (SE) Program​

Manages:

  • Inventory
  • Maintenance
  • Inspections
  • Calibration
  • Allowances (IMRL)

Ensures support equipment is safe and available.

Aircraft cannot be maintained without SE.

111.12 Discuss the Configuration Management Program​

Tracks:

  • Installed components
  • Modifications
  • TD compliance
  • Aircraft configuration

Ensures aircraft match approved design and records.

Prevents unauthorized or unsafe configurations.

111.13 Discuss the Technical Directive (TD) Compliance Program​

Ensures:

  • Required inspections/modifications completed
  • Compliance tracked
  • Records updated

TDs correct safety, reliability, or performance issues.

Mandatory compliance protects fleet safety.

111.14 Discuss the Logs and Records Program​

Maintains:

  • Aircraft logbooks
  • Component history
  • Inspection records
  • Life limits

Purpose: Provide legal and technical proof of airworthiness.

If it isn’t logged, it didn’t happen.

111.15 Discuss the Weight and Balance Program​

Maintains:

  • Current aircraft weight
  • CG calculations
  • Equipment changes

Ensures aircraft operate within safe flight limits.

Improper balance creates flight hazards.

111.16 Discuss the Plane Captain Program​

Plane Captains:

  • Conduct daily inspections
  • Service aircraft
  • Maintain cleanliness
  • Report discrepancies
  • Ensure safe for flight

They provide day-to-day aircraft custody and condition awareness.

111.17 Discuss the Maintenance Control Program​

Maintenance Control:

  • Tracks aircraft status
  • Prioritizes work
  • Coordinates parts and manpower
  • Maintains status boards

Purpose: Centralized control of maintenance production.

It is the hub of operations.

111.18 Discuss the Cannibalization Program​

Controls removal of parts from one aircraft to support another.

Requires:

  • Authorization
  • Documentation
  • Tracking

Used only when necessary.

Excessive cannibalization indicates supply or planning issues.

111.19 Discuss the Contractor Maintenance Program​

Ensures contractor work:

  • Meets standards
  • Is documented
  • Is inspected
  • Follows NAMP procedures

Government retains oversight and accountability.

111.20 Discuss the Inspection Program​

Includes:

  • Daily/turnaround
  • Special inspections
  • Phase inspections
  • Conditional inspections

Ensures early detection of discrepancies.

Preventive maintenance improves reliability.

111.21 Discuss the Maintenance Audit Program​

Periodic reviews verify:

  • Program effectiveness
  • Compliance
  • Training
  • Documentation

Identifies weaknesses and corrective actions.

Supports continuous improvement.

111.22 Discuss the Preservation and Storage Program​

Protects aircraft/components during:

  • Storage
  • Deployment
  • Inactivity

Uses:

  • Covers
  • Preservation materials
  • Periodic checks

Prevents deterioration and corrosion.

111.23 Discuss the Mishap/Discrepancy Reporting Program​

Requires:

  • Reporting hazards
  • Submitting HAZREPs
  • Documenting near misses
  • Trend tracking

Purpose: Prevent recurrence and improve safety.

Lessons learned protect the fleet.

111.24 Discuss the importance of program documentation​

Documentation:

  • Proves compliance
  • Supports audits
  • Protects legally
  • Ensures continuity

Incomplete documentation equals non-compliance.

111.25 Discuss the responsibilities of an Aviation Maintenance Officer for maintenance programs​

The AMDO:

  • Enforces NAMP compliance
  • Oversees program managers
  • Conducts audits
  • Reviews trends
  • Allocates resources
  • Trains personnel

Bottom line: Programs exist on paper, but leaders make them work.

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "Why are maintenance programs important?"

Answer:

"They standardize maintenance execution, reduce risk, and ensure safe, compliant, and ready aircraft. Programs provide the structure that turns policy into consistent performance."
