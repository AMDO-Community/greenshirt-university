118 Aviation Maintenance Administration and Management Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Administration enables execution.

Without proper:

  • Planning
  • Manning
  • Documentation
  • Communication
  • Inspections
  • Program management

Maintenance becomes reactive and inefficient.

As an AMDO, you are not only a technician.
You are a manager of people, processes, and resources.

Good administration creates predictable readiness.

118.1 Discuss the purpose of maintenance administration​

Maintenance administration provides:

  • Organization
  • Structure
  • Accountability
  • Standardization

Purpose: Ensure maintenance operations run efficiently and in compliance with policy.

118.2 Discuss responsibilities of the Maintenance Officer (MO)​

The MO:

  • Leads the maintenance department
  • Advises the CO on readiness
  • Allocates resources
  • Enforces NAMP compliance
  • Oversees safety and quality
  • Sets priorities

Responsible for overall aircraft material readiness.

118.3 Discuss responsibilities of the Assistant Maintenance Officer (AMO)​

The AMO:

  • Assists the MO
  • Supervises daily production
  • Coordinates divisions
  • Tracks readiness metrics
  • Resolves constraints

Acts as the primary execution lead.

118.4 Discuss responsibilities of Division Officers​

Division Officers:

  • Lead Sailors
  • Manage programs
  • Track qualifications
  • Supervise maintenance
  • Maintain accountability

Responsible for readiness of assigned work centers.

118.5 Discuss responsibilities of Chief Petty Officers in maintenance administration​

Chiefs:

  • Provide deckplate leadership
  • Enforce standards
  • Train personnel
  • Maintain discipline
  • Ensure safe execution

They translate policy into daily action.

118.6 Discuss manpower management​

Includes:

  • Billet alignment
  • Skill matching
  • Watchbills
  • Workload distribution

Purpose: Right Sailor, right skill, right job.

Proper manpower improves efficiency and safety.

118.7 Discuss workload planning​

Planning considers:

  • Flight schedule
  • Inspections
  • Parts availability
  • Manpower
  • Training

Purpose: Prevent bottlenecks and reduce downtime.

Proactive planning increases readiness.

118.8 Discuss maintenance scheduling tools​

Tools include:

  • Daily plans
  • Weekly schedules
  • Status boards
  • Production meetings
  • Information systems

Provide visibility and coordination.

Enable informed decisions.

118.9 Discuss correspondence and written communication​

Includes:

  • Instructions
  • Local procedures
  • Memos
  • Reports
  • Emails

Must be:

  • Clear
  • Accurate
  • Professional

Ensures consistent execution and accountability.

118.10 Discuss local command procedures (LCPs)​

LCPs:

  • Standardize local practices
  • Clarify responsibilities
  • Supplement higher instructions

Purpose: Ensure consistent and safe operations within the command.

118.11 Discuss maintenance meetings​

Examples:

  • Production meetings
  • QA meetings
  • Training meetings
  • Safety meetings

Purpose: Share information, coordinate actions, and resolve issues.

Communication improves effectiveness.

118.12 Discuss inspection preparation and administration​

Requires:

  • Program binders
  • Documentation accuracy
  • Training records
  • Self-assessments

Purpose: Demonstrate compliance and readiness.

Preparation should be continuous.

118.13 Discuss program management​

Program managers:

  • Track compliance
  • Maintain records
  • Conduct self-audits
  • Report status

Purpose: Ensure assigned programs remain effective.

Programs must be actively managed, not just assigned.

118.14 Discuss records management​

Includes:

  • Logs
  • Training records
  • Supply records
  • Program documentation

Must be:

  • Organized
  • Secure
  • Accessible

Supports audits and accountability.

118.15 Discuss reports used by maintenance leadership​

Examples:

  • Readiness reports
  • Manpower reports
  • AWP metrics
  • QA trends
  • Safety statistics

Purpose: Provide data for decision making.

Metrics guide leadership actions.

118.16 Discuss risk management in administrative decisions​

Leaders must consider:

  • Manpower limits
  • Training gaps
  • Schedule pressure
  • Resource constraints

Purpose: Avoid decisions that increase operational risk.

Administrative choices affect safety and readiness.

118.17 Discuss budget and resource management​

Includes:

  • OPTAR funding
  • Parts usage
  • Consumables
  • Local purchases

Purpose: Ensure resources are used efficiently and legally.

Good stewardship preserves readiness.

118.18 Discuss time management​

Leaders must:

  • Prioritize tasks
  • Delegate effectively
  • Focus on high impact issues

Poor time management causes delays and missed requirements.

Efficient time use improves production.

118.19 Discuss accountability of personnel and equipment​

Includes:

  • Muster
  • Custody logs
  • Inventories
  • Tool control

Purpose: Prevent loss, misuse, and safety issues.

Accountability protects resources.

118.20 Discuss continuity and turnover procedures​

Turnover must include:

  • Status brief
  • Outstanding tasks
  • Program issues
  • Risks

Ensures uninterrupted operations.

Prevents loss of critical knowledge.

118.21 Discuss training administration​

Includes:

  • Scheduling schools
  • Tracking qualifications
  • Updating records
  • Monitoring deficiencies

Ensures workforce remains qualified.

Training directly supports readiness.

118.22 Discuss communication with external organizations​

Includes coordination with:

  • Supply
  • Operations
  • Higher headquarters
  • Supporting activities

Purpose: Ensure resources and information flow effectively.

External coordination impacts readiness.

118.23 Discuss leadership climate and professionalism​

Leaders must:

  • Set standards
  • Enforce discipline
  • Promote integrity
  • Develop Sailors

Professional climate improves morale and performance.

Good leadership increases readiness.

118.24 Discuss continuous improvement​

Includes:

  • Lessons learned
  • Trend analysis
  • Process refinement
  • Feedback

Purpose: Increase efficiency and reduce errors over time.

Always seek better methods.

118.25 Discuss the responsibilities of an Aviation Maintenance Officer for maintenance administration​

The AMDO must:

  • Lead personnel
  • Manage programs
  • Allocate resources
  • Ensure compliance
  • Monitor readiness
  • Communicate effectively

Bottom line: Effective administration enables safe, efficient, and ready maintenance operations.

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "Why is maintenance administration important?"

Answer:

"Because planning, manpower management, documentation, and program control create the structure that allows maintenance to run efficiently and safely. Good administration directly improves readiness."
