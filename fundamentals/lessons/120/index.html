120 Aviation Maintenance Leadership and Professional Responsibilities Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Programs, parts, and tools do not create readiness.

People do.

Leadership determines:

  • Safety
  • Discipline
  • Standards
  • Morale
  • Performance
  • Readiness

As an Aviation Maintenance Officer, you are responsible for:

  • Mission accomplishment
  • Sailor development
  • Compliance
  • Accountability
  • Ethical decisions

Your leadership directly impacts aircraft availability and safety.

120.1 Discuss the role of an Aviation Maintenance Officer as a leader​

The AMDO:

  • Leads Sailors and civilians
  • Sets standards
  • Enforces policies
  • Allocates resources
  • Makes risk decisions

Responsible for safe, effective maintenance execution.

Leadership influences all outcomes.

120.2 Discuss the importance of command climate​

Command climate affects:

  • Morale
  • Discipline
  • Communication
  • Trust
  • Performance

Positive climate:

  • Encourages reporting
  • Promotes professionalism
  • Improves readiness

Poor climate increases errors and mishaps.

120.3 Discuss accountability​

Accountability requires:

  • Ownership of actions
  • Accurate documentation
  • Proper supervision
  • Ethical behavior

Ensures trust and reliability within the organization.

Without accountability, standards degrade.

120.4 Discuss standards enforcement​

Standards must be:

  • Clearly defined
  • Consistently applied
  • Fairly enforced

Leaders must correct deviations immediately.

Inconsistent enforcement creates unsafe practices.

120.5 Discuss integrity in maintenance leadership​

Integrity requires:

  • Honest reporting
  • Accurate documentation
  • Ethical decisions
  • Refusal to falsify data

Integrity protects:

  • Personnel
  • Aircraft
  • Command credibility

False records create safety hazards.

120.6 Discuss decision making responsibilities​

Leaders must:

  • Gather facts
  • Assess risk
  • Consider resources
  • Make timely decisions

Delays or poor decisions reduce readiness and increase risk.

Decisions must balance mission and safety.

120.7 Discuss risk acceptance authority​

Only appropriate leaders may accept operational risk.

Risk must be:

  • Deliberate
  • Informed
  • Justified

Leaders must never accept unnecessary risk.

Safety cannot be traded for convenience.

120.8 Discuss supervision requirements​

Effective supervision includes:

  • Monitoring work
  • Ensuring compliance
  • Coaching personnel
  • Verifying quality

Proper oversight prevents errors and mishaps.

Trust does not replace supervision.

120.9 Discuss communication responsibilities​

Leaders must:

  • Provide clear guidance
  • Share priorities
  • Encourage feedback
  • Report issues promptly

Poor communication causes confusion and delays.

Clear communication improves efficiency.

120.10 Discuss mentoring and development of Sailors​

Leaders must:

  • Train juniors
  • Provide feedback
  • Develop skills
  • Encourage qualifications
  • Prepare future leaders

Developed Sailors improve readiness and morale.

Leadership includes teaching.

120.11 Discuss professionalism​

Professionalism includes:

  • Bearing
  • Competence
  • Respect
  • Discipline
  • Ethical conduct

Sets the example for the organization.

Professional leaders earn trust.

120.12 Discuss time management for leaders​

Requires:

  • Prioritization
  • Delegation
  • Focus on high impact tasks

Poor time management creates missed deadlines and reactive leadership.

Efficient leaders improve production.

120.13 Discuss delegation​

Delegation:

  • Assigns responsibility
  • Develops subordinates
  • Improves efficiency

Leaders must:

  • Provide clear expectations
  • Monitor results

Cannot delegate accountability.

120.14 Discuss conflict resolution​

Leaders must:

  • Address issues early
  • Listen to all parties
  • Apply fair solutions
  • Maintain professionalism

Unresolved conflict harms morale and performance.

Early intervention preserves teamwork.

120.15 Discuss discipline​

Discipline:

  • Ensures standards
  • Promotes safety
  • Maintains order

Leaders must:

  • Correct deficiencies
  • Apply consequences fairly

Consistent discipline improves readiness.

120.16 Discuss counseling and feedback​

Includes:

  • Formal counseling
  • Informal feedback
  • Performance evaluations

Purpose: Guide improvement and reinforce strengths.

Regular feedback improves performance.

120.17 Discuss workload management​

Leaders must:

  • Balance tasks
  • Avoid burnout
  • Monitor fatigue
  • Adjust priorities

Overworked personnel make mistakes.

Proper workload supports safety.

120.18 Discuss ethical responsibilities​

Leaders must:

  • Follow regulations
  • Protect resources
  • Avoid conflicts of interest
  • Report violations

Ethical conduct maintains trust and legality.

Unethical behavior damages readiness and careers.

120.19 Discuss stewardship of government resources​

Includes:

  • Tools
  • Equipment
  • Parts
  • Funding
  • Manpower

Leaders must ensure efficient, lawful use.

Waste reduces capability.

120.20 Discuss readiness reporting responsibilities​

Reports must be:

  • Accurate
  • Timely
  • Honest

False reporting misleads leadership and creates operational risk.

Integrity in reporting is mandatory.

120.21 Discuss safety leadership​

Leaders must:

  • Enforce ORM
  • Stop unsafe acts
  • Promote reporting
  • Set example

Safety culture begins with leadership.

Safe commands maintain higher readiness.

120.22 Discuss leading during high tempo operations​

Requires:

  • Clear priorities
  • Fatigue management
  • Increased supervision
  • Risk assessment

High tempo increases error probability.

Leaders must slow down enough to stay safe.

120.23 Discuss continuous improvement mindset​

Leaders should:

  • Analyze trends
  • Seek feedback
  • Improve processes
  • Eliminate inefficiencies

Constant improvement increases readiness over time.

Never accept “good enough.”

120.24 Discuss the relationship between leadership and readiness​

Good leadership:

  • Improves morale
  • Reduces errors
  • Increases efficiency
  • Enhances safety

Poor leadership:

  • Creates confusion
  • Increases mishaps
  • Reduces availability

Leadership directly drives readiness.

120.25 Discuss the responsibilities of an Aviation Maintenance Officer for leadership and professional conduct​

The AMDO must:

  • Lead by example
  • Enforce standards
  • Develop Sailors
  • Make ethical decisions
  • Protect resources
  • Ensure safe, compliant maintenance

Bottom line: Leadership is the primary factor that determines maintenance success.

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "What is the most important responsibility of an AMDO?"

Answer:

"Leadership. Because programs, parts, and processes only work when people are properly led, trained, and held accountable. Effective leadership directly determines safety and readiness."
