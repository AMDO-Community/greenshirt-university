102 Aviation Fundamentals

102.1 Aerodynamic terms and relationships​

Lift​

Upward force created by airflow over wings/rotors that opposes weight.

Weight​

Force of gravity acting on the aircraft downward.

Drag​

Resistance to motion through the air.

Thrust​

Forward force produced by engines or propellers.

Longitudinal axis​

Nose to tail, roll occurs about this axis.

Vertical axis​

Top to bottom, yaw occurs about this axis.

Lateral axis​

Wingtip to wingtip, pitch occurs about this axis.

Angle of Attack (AOA)​

Angle between relative wind and wing chord line. Increasing AOA increases lift until stall.

Relationship​

For steady flight: Lift = Weight and Thrust = Drag.

102.2 Three primary aircraft movements​

  • Roll
  • Pitch
  • Yaw

102.3 Purpose of flight control surfaces​

  • Flaps - increase lift/drag for takeoff and landing
  • Spoilers - reduce lift and increase drag
  • Speed brakes slow aircraft
  • Slats - delay stall
  • Horizontal stabilizer - pitch stability
  • Vertical stabilizer - yaw stability
  • Rudder - yaw control
  • Main rotor - lift and movement
  • Tail rotor - counter torque/yaw
  • Ailerons - roll
  • Elevator - pitch
  • Leading edge flaps - improved low speed lift

102.4 Primary controls​

Fixed wing​

Stick/yoke, rudder pedals, throttle

Rotary wing​

Cyclic, collective, anti torque pedals

Tilt rotor​

Combination of rotor and fixed wing controls

102.5 Purpose of instruments​

  • Pitot static, measures airspeed/altitude
  • Airspeed indicator, speed through air
  • Altimeter, altitude above sea level
  • Rate of climb, vertical speed
  • Attitude indicator, aircraft orientation
  • Turn and bank, rate of turn/slip
  • Navigation systems, position guidance
  • Compass, heading reference
  • Communication systems, coordination
  • Accelerometers, G forces

102.6 Basic hydraulic/pneumatic components​

  • Pump/compressor
  • Reservoir
  • Lines
  • Valves
  • Actuators

Used to transmit power for brakes, gear, and flight controls.

102.7 Landing gear components​

Shock struts, wheels, brakes, retraction system.
Absorb impact and allow taxi/landing operations.

102.8 Autorotation​

Rotor driven by upward airflow rather than engine power.
Allows controlled emergency landing after power loss.

102.9 Retreating blade stall​

Occurs when retreating rotor blade loses lift at high speed, causing vibration and roll tendency.

102.10 Power settling (Vortex Ring State)​

Aircraft descends into its own downwash, losing lift.
Common during high rate of descent with low airspeed.

102.11 Icing effects​

Adds weight, increases drag, reduces lift, disrupts airflow, and may cause engine/compressor issues.

102.12 Ground effect​

Fixed wing​

Reduced drag near ground, improved lift

Rotary wing​

More efficient hover due to reduced induced flow

102.13 Five jet engine sections​

  • Intake
  • Compressor
  • Combustor
  • Turbine
  • Exhaust

102.14 Engine types​

  • Turboprop, prop driven, efficient low speed
  • Turbojet, high speed thrust, fuel heavy
  • Turbofan, efficient, high bypass
  • Turboshaft, drives rotor/gearbox
  • APU, auxiliary power

102.15 Afterburner purpose​

Injects fuel into exhaust to increase thrust for short periods.

102.17 Effects of overstress​

Fatigue cracking, structural weakening, shortened service life.

102.18 Fuel characteristics​

  • JP4, low flashpoint, more volatile
  • JP5, higher flashpoint, safer for ships
  • JP8, similar to JP5, common joint fuel

102.19 Aviation community missions (summary)​

  • HSC/HSM, helicopter combat/ASW
  • VFA/VMFA, strike fighter
  • VP/VQ, patrol/recon
  • VR/VRC, logistics
  • VT/HT, training
  • VX/HX, test and evaluation

102.20 Aircraft missions (general knowledge)​

  • Fighters, air superiority/strike
  • Patrol aircraft, ISR and ASW
  • Helicopters, logistics/rescue/ASW
  • Transports, personnel/cargo
  • UAVs, ISR

(Officers should know primary mission of each assigned platform)

102.21 Radar principles​

Uses radio waves to detect range, bearing, altitude, and speed of objects.

102.22 IFF​

Identifies friendly aircraft through coded transponder responses.

102.23 INS​

Provides position, velocity, and attitude without external signals.

102.24 Ship aviation missions​

  • CVN, carrier air operations
  • LHA/LHD, amphibious aviation
  • DDG/CG, helo support
  • Others, logistics or specialty roles

102.25 Aviation ratings purpose​

Each rating provides specialized maintenance or operational support, such as:

  • AD engines
  • AE electrical
  • AM structures
  • AT avionics
  • AO ordnance
  • PR aircrew survival
  • LS supply
  • AZ admin

102.26 Electrical schematics/block diagrams​

Show system layout, signal flow, and troubleshooting paths.

102.27 Wiring identification​

Ensures correct routing, troubleshooting, and maintenance.

102.28 Tool purposes​

  • Circuit breaker/fuse, overcurrent protection
  • Multimeter, voltage/current/resistance
  • TDR, cable faults
  • Megger, insulation testing
  • Oscilloscope, signal visualization

102.29 Electrical basics​

  • Voltage, electrical pressure
  • Current, flow of electrons
  • Resistance, opposition
  • Ohm’s Law, V = IR

102.30 Night ops lighting​

Preserve night vision and prevent FOD or collision hazards.

102.31 Intake/exhaust hazards​

Suction, jet blast, burns, and FOD ingestion.

102.32 Hot exhaust effects​

Damages ordnance, equipment, and personnel.

102.33 JBD hazards​

Extreme heat and pressure behind aircraft at high power.

102.34 Rotor hazards​

Strikes and downwash can injure personnel and move equipment.

102.35 Flight deck hazards​

Catapult tracks, foul lines, moving aircraft.

102.36 Tie downs​

Chains/chocks prevent aircraft movement.

102.37 Engine ingestion​

Hot exhaust ingestion reduces performance and damages engines.

102.38 Recovery dangers​

Landing area is high risk for personnel due to moving aircraft/arresting gear.

102.39 Personnel movement​

Stay clear of taxi paths, follow directors, maintain situational awareness.

102.40 Protective equipment​

Covers, screens, FOD control devices protect aircraft and systems.

102.43 Jersey colors​

  • Green, maintenance
  • Yellow, directors
  • Red, ordnance/crash
  • Brown, plane captains
  • Blue, chock/chain
  • Purple, fuels
  • White, safety/medical/QA

102.45 Fire classes​

  • A, ordinary materials
  • B, flammable liquids
  • C, electrical
  • D, metals

Use proper extinguishing agents.

102.47 Crash and salvage crew​

Firefighting, rescue, aircraft recovery, hazard control.

102.48 Support equipment hazards​

Heavy equipment, high pressure gases, electricity, hydraulics.
Proper training and PPE required.

102.49 Air department responsibilities​

Air Boss, flight deck, fuels, handling, and crash teams coordinate safe flight ops.

102.55 Weather terms​

  • Weather, short term conditions
  • Climate, long term patterns
  • Tropical cyclone, rotating storm system

102.57 Conditions of readiness​

Different levels of preparation for severe weather to protect ship and aircraft.
