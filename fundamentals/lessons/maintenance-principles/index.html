110 Basic Aviation Supply Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Supply drives readiness.

Most aircraft downtime is caused by parts availability, not wrench turning.

As a maintenance leader, you must understand how to:

  • Order parts
  • Track status
  • Interpret codes
  • Manage repairables
  • Work with ASD
  • Reduce AWP

If you cannot speak supply, you cannot fix readiness.

110.1 State the function and responsibilities of Material Control​

Material Control coordinates all maintenance-related material requirements.

Responsibilities include:

  • Validating parts requirements
  • Submitting requisitions
  • Assigning priorities
  • Tracking due-ins and AWP
  • Coordinating with Supply
  • Expediting NMCS/PMCS parts
  • Maintaining material logs and records
  • Ensuring proper turn-in of repairables

Purpose: Ensure the right part is available at the right time to support maintenance.

110.2 Discuss the following OPTAR funding and give examples​

a. Flight Operations Fund (OFC-01)​

Funds day-to-day flight operations support.

Examples:

  • Aviation fuel related support items
  • Line consumables
  • Ground handling consumables
  • Launch/recovery support items
  • Operational support materials

b. Aviation Fleet Maintenance Fund (OFC-50)​

Funds aircraft and support equipment maintenance.

Examples:

  • Repair parts
  • Consumables
  • Lubricants/sealants
  • Support equipment parts
  • Maintenance related materials

110.3 State procedures for the following​

a. Ordering parts and material​

  • Identify requirement
  • Verify correct NSN/PN
  • Assign priority designator
  • Select proper funding
  • Submit requisition through system
  • Track status

b. Receipt and delivery​

  • Verify item and quantity
  • Inspect condition
  • Post receipt in system
  • Notify work center
  • Deliver to customer

c. Turn-in of defective components​

  • Remove and document discrepancy
  • Tag and package properly
  • Process required paperwork
  • Turn in to Supply/I-level
  • Update tracking system

110.4 Define MILSTRIP and state its purpose​

MILSTRIP (Military Standard Requisitioning and Issue Procedures) is the standardized DoD process and format used to requisition, issue, and track material.

Purpose: Ensure uniform supply communication across all services.

110.9 Discuss the purpose of the supplementary address​

Designates an alternate delivery or accounting address.

Used when material must ship to a location other than the requisitioner.

J – Priority air

7 – Expedited
9 – Emergency

110.12 Explain the importance of the following allowance lists​

AVCAL​

Shipboard aviation spares allowance

SHORCAL​

Shore activity allowance

COSAL​

Ship’s consolidated allowance list

Purpose: Ensure required parts are on hand to reduce downtime.

110.13 Explain the purpose of SM&R codes and define​

Purpose: Identify source, repair level, and disposition.

PAOGD – O-level remove, depot repair
PAOZZ – O-level discard
PAODD – Depot repair/disposition

110.14 State the purpose of the following forms and reports​

DD 200 – Property loss investigation
SF 44 – Local purchase
DoD AIRCARD – Fuel/services payment
DD 1348 – Requisition/issue document

110.15 Explain the mission and responsibilities of ASD​

Provides:

  • Parts
  • Consumables
  • Repairables
  • Inventory management
  • Financial accountability

Purpose: Enable maintenance readiness.

110.16 Discuss repairable management​

Tracks high value components through:

  • Issue
  • Turn-in
  • Repair
  • Reissue

Ensures accountability and credit return.

110.17 Discuss AWP management​

Tracks parts that delay maintenance.

Goal: Reduce NMCS/PMCS time through expediting and prioritization.

110.18 Discuss priority designators​

Assigned using UMMIPS.

Higher priority equals faster processing and shipment.

Used for NMCS/PMCS and safety-of-flight items.

110.19 Discuss supply customer responsibilities​

Customers must:

  • Submit accurate requisitions
  • Use correct data
  • Turn in repairables promptly
  • Track status
  • Communicate urgency

110.20 Discuss inventory accuracy importance​

Accurate inventory:

  • Prevents false stock
  • Reduces delays
  • Supports readiness

Inaccurate data causes downtime and wasted funds.

110.21 Discuss local purchase procedures​

Used when item is unavailable through normal supply.

Requires:

  • Authorization
  • Funding
  • Proper documentation

Provides rapid acquisition for urgent needs.

110.22 Discuss financial accountability​

All material and funds must be tracked.

Requires:

  • Proper documentation
  • Custody control
  • Auditable records

Protects government resources.

110.23 Discuss supply support of deployed operations​

Includes:

  • Pre-deployment allowances
  • High demand spares
  • Rapid resupply
  • Coordination with ship or host site

Goal: Sustain readiness away from home station.

110.24 Discuss maintenance and supply coordination​

Maintenance identifies requirements.
Supply sources and delivers material.

Close communication minimizes downtime and improves turnaround time.

110.25 Discuss the role of an Aviation Maintenance Officer in supply management​

The AMDO:

  • Monitors AWP/NMCS
  • Works closely with ASD
  • Prioritizes parts
  • Reviews trends
  • Removes logistics barriers

Bottom line:​

Effective supply management directly increases aircraft availability.
