119 Aviation Maintenance Planning, Logistics Integration, and Deployment Readiness Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Planning determines readiness before operations begin.

Good planning:

  • Prevents AWP
  • Reduces downtime
  • Aligns manpower
  • Stages parts
  • Anticipates risk

Poor planning creates:

  • Broken schedules
  • Cannibalization
  • Fatigue
  • Unsafe decisions

As an AMDO, you must integrate: Maintenance + Supply + Manpower + Operations

Planning turns capability into combat power.

119.1 Discuss the purpose of maintenance planning​

Maintenance planning organizes:

  • Workload
  • Resources
  • Time
  • Risk

Purpose: Ensure aircraft and equipment are ready to meet mission requirements.

Planning reduces reactive maintenance.

119.2 Discuss short-range maintenance planning​

Short-range planning focuses on:

  • Daily schedule
  • Next flight events
  • Immediate discrepancies
  • Manpower allocation

Purpose: Execute current operations safely and efficiently.

119.3 Discuss mid-range maintenance planning​

Mid-range planning focuses on:

  • Weekly schedules
  • Inspections
  • Parts staging
  • Training requirements

Purpose: Balance workload and prevent bottlenecks.

119.4 Discuss long-range maintenance planning​

Long-range planning focuses on:

  • Phase inspections
  • Major component changes
  • Deployment cycles
  • Resource forecasts

Purpose: Prepare the command for future operational demands.

119.5 Discuss integration of maintenance and operations schedules​

Requires coordination between:

  • Maintenance Control
  • Operations
  • Supply

Ensures:

  • Realistic flight schedules
  • Adequate downtime
  • Safe execution

Uncoordinated schedules increase risk.

119.6 Discuss parts staging and pre-expended bins (PEBs)​

Parts staging:

  • Orders parts before scheduled maintenance
  • Positions high use consumables locally

PEBs:

  • Pre-approved consumables for rapid issue

Purpose: Reduce maintenance delays and turnaround time.

119.7 Discuss manpower planning​

Includes:

  • Skill mix
  • Watchbills
  • Leave schedules
  • Training
  • Deployment manning

Purpose: Ensure sufficient qualified personnel are available.

Manpower shortages reduce readiness.

119.8 Discuss detachment planning​

Requires:

  • Personnel selection
  • Tool allowance
  • SE requirements
  • Parts pack-ups
  • Documentation transfer

Purpose: Maintain full maintenance capability away from home station.

119.9 Discuss pack-up kits (PUKs)​

PUKs contain:

  • High demand parts
  • Consumables
  • Repairables

Pre-positioned to support detachments and deployments.

Purpose: Reduce AWP in remote locations.

119.10 Discuss allowance lists for deployment​

Includes:

  • AVCAL
  • SHORCAL
  • IMRL
  • Tool allowances

Purpose: Ensure required material and equipment accompany the unit.

Proper allowances prevent shortages.

119.11 Discuss supply support coordination​

Requires:

  • Early requisitions
  • Status tracking
  • Expediting
  • Communication with ASD and higher supply

Purpose: Maintain logistics flow during operations.

Supply drives readiness.

119.12 Discuss support equipment (SE) planning​

Includes:

  • Required SE inventory
  • Calibration status
  • Maintenance readiness
  • Transportation requirements

Purpose: Ensure ability to perform maintenance at deployed site.

Aircraft cannot be maintained without SE.

119.13 Discuss tool control during deployments​

Requires:

  • Inventories
  • Custody logs
  • Shadow boards
  • Accountability

Purpose: Prevent FOD and loss.

Tools must remain controlled outside home station.

119.14 Discuss readiness reporting during operations​

Reports include:

  • Aircraft status
  • Parts availability
  • Manpower readiness
  • SE status

Purpose: Provide leadership situational awareness.

Accurate reporting supports decision making.

119.15 Discuss contingency planning​

Includes planning for:

  • Parts shortages
  • Weather impacts
  • Equipment failure
  • Personnel loss
  • Surge operations

Purpose: Maintain capability under unexpected conditions.

Plan for failure points.

119.16 Discuss cannibalization planning considerations​

Cannibalization may be used when:

  • Parts unavailable
  • Mission critical need exists

Must be:

  • Authorized
  • Documented
  • Controlled

Frequent use indicates poor logistics planning.

119.17 Discuss AWP reduction strategies​

Includes:

  • Early requisitions
  • Accurate forecasting
  • Local stock optimization
  • Expediting
  • Repairable management

Reducing AWP increases mission capable rates.

119.18 Discuss maintenance scheduling around inspections​

Requires:

  • Downtime windows
  • Parts availability
  • Qualified personnel
  • Risk assessment

Purpose: Prevent last-minute delays and schedule disruptions.

119.19 Discuss coordination with intermediate/depot level maintenance​

Includes:

  • Repairables
  • Calibration
  • Component repair
  • Technical support

Purpose: Ensure higher level maintenance support is available when needed.

External support impacts readiness.

119.20 Discuss transportation planning for maintenance assets​

Includes:

  • Aircraft movement
  • SE transport
  • Parts shipment
  • Hazardous material handling

Purpose: Ensure required assets arrive safely and on time.

Transportation delays affect readiness.

119.21 Discuss documentation transfer during deployments​

Requires:

  • Logbooks
  • Records
  • MAF history
  • Training records

Purpose: Maintain continuity and accountability.

Incomplete records create risk.

119.22 Discuss communications planning​

Includes:

  • Maintenance reporting
  • Status updates
  • Coordination with higher headquarters
  • Secure communication methods

Purpose: Maintain situational awareness and support.

Clear communication prevents delays.

119.23 Discuss risk management in operational planning​

Uses ORM to evaluate:

  • Manpower fatigue
  • Equipment limitations
  • Schedule pressure
  • Environmental hazards

Purpose: Balance mission accomplishment with safety.

Risk must be deliberate.

119.24 Discuss measures of maintenance effectiveness​

Examples:

  • MC rate
  • Sortie completion rate
  • AWP days
  • TAT
  • Repeat discrepancies

Metrics assess planning effectiveness.

Used to adjust processes.

119.25 Discuss the responsibilities of an Aviation Maintenance Officer for maintenance planning and logistics integration​

The Aviation Maintenance Officer must:

  • Plan maintenance workload
  • Coordinate supply and logistics
  • Stage resources
  • Align manpower
  • Anticipate risks
  • Support deployments
  • Monitor readiness metrics

Bottom line: Effective planning ensures aircraft are ready before the mission begins.

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "Why is maintenance planning critical?"

Answer:

"Because readiness is determined before operations begin. Proper planning aligns manpower, parts, and equipment so aircraft are available safely and on time."
