116 Naval Aviation Training and Qualification Programs Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Training equals readiness.

Untrained personnel create:

  • Mishaps
  • Rework
  • Delays
  • False sign-offs
  • Reduced readiness

As an AMDO, you are responsible for ensuring:

  • Personnel are trained
  • Qualifications are documented
  • Certifications are current
  • Only qualified Sailors perform maintenance

If it is not documented, they are not qualified.

Right Sailor. Right skill. Right job.

116.1 Discuss the purpose of the command training program​

The command training program ensures personnel:

  • Receive required training
  • Maintain qualifications
  • Develop technical proficiency
  • Support mission readiness

Purpose: Provide a safe, capable, and combat ready workforce.

116.2 Discuss the purpose of the Personnel Qualification Standards (PQS) program​

PQS:

  • Defines required knowledge and skills
  • Standardizes qualifications
  • Provides task-based training
  • Documents completion

Purpose: Ensure personnel meet minimum competency standards.

116.3 Discuss responsibilities of the Training Officer​

The Training Officer:

  • Manages training program
  • Tracks qualifications
  • Schedules schools
  • Maintains records
  • Coordinates with leadership

Ensures command meets all training requirements.

116.4 Discuss responsibilities of Division Officers for training​

Division Officers:

  • Monitor Sailor qualifications
  • Ensure PQS progress
  • Schedule training
  • Identify gaps
  • Develop personnel

Responsible for readiness of their division.

116.5 Discuss responsibilities of Work Center Supervisors for training​

Supervisors:

  • Provide on-the-job training
  • Validate task performance
  • Recommend certifications
  • Ensure safe practices
  • Track daily proficiency

They are the primary trainers at the deckplate level.

116.6 Discuss the purpose of On-the-Job Training (OJT)​

OJT:

  • Provides hands-on experience
  • Reinforces classroom instruction
  • Builds practical skills

Purpose: Develop real-world maintenance proficiency.

Learning by doing.

116.7 Discuss the purpose of formal schools​

Formal schools provide:

  • Standardized instruction
  • Technical knowledge
  • Certifications
  • NECs

Purpose: Establish baseline competence across the fleet.

116.8 Discuss the purpose of Navy Enlisted Classifications (NECs)​

NECs identify:

  • Specialized skills
  • Advanced qualifications
  • Unique capabilities

Used to:

  • Assign personnel
  • Fill billets
  • Match skills to requirements

Ensures proper skill alignment.

116.9 Discuss the Training Jacket/Training Record​

Tracks:

  • Schools
  • Certifications
  • PQS
  • Licenses
  • Qualifications

Purpose: Official record of training history.

Provides proof of qualification.

116.10 Discuss the purpose of FLTMPS​

Fleet Training Management and Planning System tracks:

  • Training completion
  • Qualifications
  • Certifications
  • Schools

Purpose: Centralized, authoritative training record for the command.

Supports inspections and readiness assessments.

116.11 Discuss the purpose of ASM (Advanced Skills Management)​

ASM tracks:

  • Individual skills
  • Qualifications
  • Watchstations
  • Certifications

Purpose: Provide visibility of command skill sets and training gaps.

Supports manpower and readiness planning.

116.12 Discuss certification requirements for maintenance personnel​

Only certified personnel may:

  • Inspect
  • Sign off
  • Perform specific maintenance tasks

Certification requires:

  • Training
  • Experience
  • Supervisor recommendation
  • Documentation

Prevents unqualified maintenance actions.

116.13 Discuss Collateral Duty Inspector (CDI) certification​

CDIs:

  • Inspect specific systems
  • Verify maintenance quality
  • Sign maintenance documentation

Require:

  • Technical expertise
  • Training
  • Command designation

Provides quality control at the work center level.

116.14 Discuss Collateral Duty Quality Assurance Representative (CDQAR) certification​

CDQARs:

  • Perform advanced inspections
  • Support QA audits
  • Provide technical oversight

Require:

  • Higher experience
  • Additional training
  • Command designation

Assist QA in ensuring compliance.

116.15 Discuss Quality Assurance Representative (QAR) certification​

QARs:

  • Full-time inspectors
  • Conduct audits and investigations
  • Certify critical maintenance actions
  • Advise leadership

Highest level of maintenance quality authority.

116.16 Discuss the importance of recurring training​

Recurring training:

  • Maintains proficiency
  • Updates procedures
  • Reinforces safety
  • Prevents complacency

Skills degrade without practice.

Continuous training preserves readiness.

116.17 Discuss cross-training​

Cross-training:

  • Expands skill sets
  • Improves flexibility
  • Reduces single points of failure

Allows coverage during shortages or deployments.

Improves resilience of the workforce.

116.18 Discuss safety training requirements​

Includes:

  • ORM
  • HAZMAT
  • Tool control
  • FOD prevention
  • PPE
  • Emergency procedures

Purpose: Reduce injuries and mishaps.

Safety is part of every job.

116.19 Discuss licensing programs (e.g., support equipment, vehicle operators)​

Licenses ensure:

  • Proper training
  • Safe operation
  • Legal authorization

Examples:

  • Tow tractors
  • SE
  • Forklifts

Only licensed personnel may operate equipment.

116.20 Discuss watchstation qualification programs​

Watchstations require:

  • PQS completion
  • Knowledge checks
  • Supervisor sign-off

Purpose: Ensure qualified personnel perform critical duties.

Protects operational safety.

116.21 Discuss training during deployments or detachments​

Requires:

  • Continued PQS progress
  • On-site OJT
  • Refresher training
  • Documentation

Ensures readiness is maintained away from home station.

116.22 Discuss tracking of training deficiencies​

Includes:

  • Identifying gaps
  • Scheduling corrective training
  • Monitoring completion

Prevents unqualified personnel from performing tasks.

Supports inspection readiness.

116.23 Discuss the relationship between training and readiness​

Well-trained personnel:

  • Work faster
  • Make fewer errors
  • Require less supervision
  • Improve aircraft availability

Training directly increases readiness.

116.24 Discuss the importance of documentation of training​

Documentation:

  • Proves qualification
  • Supports audits
  • Protects legally
  • Enables manpower planning

If not recorded, it does not count.

116.25 Discuss the responsibilities of an Aviation Maintenance Officer for training programs​

The AMDO must:

  • Ensure compliance with training requirements
  • Monitor qualifications
  • Allocate training resources
  • Develop Sailors
  • Close skill gaps
  • Maintain documentation

Bottom line: Qualified people produce safe and ready aircraft.

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "Why is training critical to maintenance readiness?"

Answer:

"Because only properly trained and certified personnel can safely and effectively maintain aircraft. Training reduces errors, improves efficiency, and directly increases readiness."
