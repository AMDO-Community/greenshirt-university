108 Organizational Structure Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Organizational structure defines:

  • Who owns what
  • Who reports to who
  • How maintenance flows
  • How decisions are made

Clear structure prevents confusion, delays, and mishaps.

As an AMDO, you must understand how maintenance, supply, QA, and operations integrate to produce ready aircraft.

108.1 Discuss the purpose of the Naval Aviation Maintenance Program (NAMP) organizational structure​

The NAMP establishes standardized maintenance organization, responsibilities, and procedures across Naval Aviation.

Purpose:

  • Safety
  • Standardization
  • Accountability
  • Readiness

It ensures every command executes maintenance the same way fleetwide.

108.2 Discuss the Maintenance Department chain of command​

Typical chain:

  • Commanding Officer
  • Executive Officer
  • Maintenance Officer (MO)
  • Assistant Maintenance Officer (AMO)
  • Division Officers
  • Chief Petty Officers
  • Work center supervisors

Each level provides increasing leadership, oversight, and accountability.

Clear authority ensures safe and efficient maintenance execution.

108.3 Discuss responsibilities of the Maintenance Officer (MO)​

The MO:

  • Overall maintenance program manager
  • Advises CO on readiness
  • Allocates manpower/resources
  • Ensures NAMP compliance
  • Oversees QA, production, and material control
  • Signs safe-for-flight policies

Responsible for total aircraft material readiness.

108.4 Discuss responsibilities of the Assistant Maintenance Officer (AMO)​

The AMO:

  • Assists MO in daily operations
  • Supervises divisions
  • Coordinates production and priorities
  • Tracks readiness metrics
  • Ensures training and qualifications

Acts as the execution lead for maintenance.

108.5 Discuss the purpose of Production Control​

Production Control:

  • Schedules maintenance
  • Prioritizes workload
  • Tracks aircraft status
  • Coordinates parts and manpower
  • Maintains maintenance control boards

Purpose: Efficient use of time and resources to maximize aircraft availability.

It is the “brain” of maintenance flow.

108.6 Discuss Quality Assurance (QA) organization and responsibilities​

QA:

  • Audits programs
  • Inspects maintenance
  • Investigates discrepancies
  • Monitors trends
  • Manages CDIs/QARs
  • Ensures compliance

Purpose: Prevent errors before they become mishaps.

QA provides independent oversight.

108.7 Discuss Material Control responsibilities​

Material Control:

  • Orders parts
  • Tracks repairables
  • Manages AWP
  • Coordinates supply
  • Maintains inventory accuracy

Purpose: Ensure the right parts are available when needed.

No parts equals no readiness.

108.8 Discuss Line Division responsibilities​

Line Division:

  • Daily/turnaround inspections
  • Launch and recovery support
  • Servicing
  • Aircraft movement
  • Minor maintenance
  • Aircraft custody

Purpose: Keep aircraft safe, fueled, and ready for flight.

They are the face of maintenance on the flight line.

108.9 Discuss Power Plants Division responsibilities​

Power Plants:

  • Engines
  • Fuel systems
  • Oil systems
  • Propulsion related components

Purpose: Maintain propulsion reliability and safety.

Engine issues directly impact mission capability.

108.10 Discuss Airframes Division responsibilities​

Airframes:

  • Structures
  • Hydraulics
  • Landing gear
  • Flight controls
  • Corrosion control

Purpose: Maintain structural integrity and safe flight characteristics.

108.11 Discuss Avionics Division responsibilities​

Avionics:

  • Electrical systems
  • Instruments
  • Navigation/communication
  • Radar
  • Mission systems

Purpose: Maintain aircraft sensors and electronics required for combat capability.

108.12 Discuss Armament Division responsibilities​

Armament:

  • Weapons systems
  • Loading/download
  • Gun systems
  • Missile launchers
  • CAD/PAD
  • Ordnance safety

Purpose: Ensure safe and reliable weapons employment.

108.13 Discuss Maintenance Control Center (MCC) functions​

MCC:

  • Central communication hub
  • Tracks aircraft status
  • Coordinates maintenance actions
  • Dispatches support
  • Maintains logs

Purpose: Real-time situational awareness of all aircraft and maintenance activity.

108.14 Discuss relationship between Maintenance and Operations Departments​

Maintenance and Operations must coordinate:

  • Flight schedule
  • Aircraft availability
  • Discrepancies
  • Priorities
  • Risk decisions

Purpose: Balance mission requirements with maintenance capability.

Good communication prevents unrealistic schedules and unsafe pressure.

108.15 Discuss relationship between Maintenance and Supply​

Maintenance depends on Supply for:

  • Parts
  • Tools
  • Repairables
  • Consumables

Supply depends on Maintenance for:

  • Accurate demand
  • Proper documentation
  • Turn-ins

Purpose: Integrated logistics support.

They function as one team.

108.16 Discuss the role of an Aviation Maintenance Officer within the organizational structure​

The Aviation Maintenance Officer:

  • Leads divisions
  • Manages manpower
  • Enforces standards
  • Coordinates with QA, Supply, and Ops
  • Balances risk and readiness
  • Develops Sailors

Bottom line: Translate command intent into safe, effective maintenance execution.

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "Why is organizational structure important?"

Answer:

"Because clear roles, responsibilities, and reporting relationships ensure maintenance is executed safely, efficiently, and consistently. Without structure, accountability breaks down and readiness suffers."
