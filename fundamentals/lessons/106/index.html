106 Aviation Ordnance Fundamentals

Big Picture​

Ordnance is inherently hazardous.

As a maintenance leader, your priorities are:

  • Safety
  • Accountability
  • Certification
  • Procedural compliance
  • Risk management

Goal: Zero mishaps while delivering combat capability.

You are not expected to be an AO, but you must understand:

  • What weapons do
  • How they are handled
  • Who is authorized
  • What programs control risk

If it goes boom, you own the risk.

106.1 Discuss the different classes of explosives​

Low explosives​

Burn rapidly (deflagration), used for propulsion. Examples: propellants, rocket motors.

High explosives​

Detonate rapidly, create shockwave. Examples: bombs, warheads, demolition charges.

Primary explosives​

Extremely sensitive, used to initiate detonation. Examples: primers, detonators.

Secondary explosives​

Less sensitive, require initiator. Examples: main charges in bombs/missiles.

106.2 Discuss ammunition compatibility for stowage​

Compatibility determines which explosives may be stored together safely.

Purpose: Prevent chain reactions.

Factors:

  • Explosive class
  • Sensitivity
  • Quantity-distance requirements
  • Magazine type

Rules: Incompatible ordnance must be segregated to reduce risk of sympathetic detonation.

106.3 Describe shipboard Weapons Department divisions​

Typical divisions:

  • G-1, Administration
  • G-2, Armament/weapons assembly
  • G-3, Aviation ordnance
  • G-4, Magazines/storage
  • G-5, Missile systems

Purpose: Safe stowage, issue, maintenance, and accountability of all weapons.

106.4 Duties of the Ordnance Handling Officer (OHO)​

The OHO:

  • Oversees ordnance evolution safety
  • Supervises loading/download
  • Ensures qualified personnel
  • Enforces procedures
  • Coordinates with Air Dept and Maintenance

Bottom line: Safety officer for all weapons movement.

106.5 Functions of the following​

Gun system (M61A1)​

Aircraft internal cannon for air-to-air/air-to-ground fire.

Rockets​

Unguided weapons for area targets.

Bombs​

Gravity delivered explosive weapons.

Laser guided bombs​

Precision guided munitions using laser designation.

Pyrotechnics​

Flares, markers, signaling devices.

Missiles​

Self-propelled guided weapons.

Mines​

Area denial or anti-ship/subsurface devices.

Torpedoes​

Underwater self-propelled anti-submarine/ship weapons.

CAD​

Cartridge Activated Device, explosive device used for ejection seats, canopy jettison, etc.

106.6 Purpose of the following missiles​

HELLFIRE (AGM-114)​

Precision anti-armor/close air support.

AMRAAM (AIM-120)​

Beyond visual range air-to-air.

SIDEWINDER (AIM-9)​

Short range IR air-to-air.

HARM (AGM-88)​

Destroys enemy radar/air defenses.

MAVERICK (AGM-65)​

Air-to-ground precision strike.

HARPOON (AGM-84)​

Anti-ship missile.

SLAM-ER​

Long-range precision land/ship strike.

JSOW​

Glide weapon for standoff precision attack.

106.7 Conventional Ordnance Performance Evaluation (COPE)​

COPE: Evaluates ordnance team proficiency.

Measures:

  • Safety compliance
  • Procedures
  • Knowledge
  • Handling skills

Purpose: Standardize training and reduce mishaps.

106.8 Ordnance publication terms​

Warning​

Risk of death/serious injury.

Caution​

Risk of equipment damage/minor injury.

Note​

Helpful information.

Shall​

Mandatory.

Should​

Recommended.

May/Need​

Optional or conditional.

Will​

Required action or expected result.

106.9 Define the following​

Airborne weapons​

Weapons carried or employed by aircraft.

Arming​

Making weapon ready to function.

Hung weapons​

Weapon fails to release or fire.

Intent to launch​

Condition where weapon could fire if commanded.

Rearming area​

Designated safe loading/download area.

Unexpended weapons​

Weapons carried but not used during mission.

106.10 Ordnance Qualification Program terms​

Board Chairman​

Leads certification board.

Board Member​

Evaluates knowledge/skills.

Certification​

Authorization to perform specific tasks.

Decertification​

Removal of qualification due to safety or performance.

Training Plan​

Structured path to qualification.

OPNAV 8020/2​

Medical clearance certifying personnel fit for explosive handling.

106.11 Define the following​

HERP​

Hazard of Electromagnetic Radiation to Personnel.

HERF​

Hazard of Electromagnetic Radiation to Fuel.

HERO​

Hazard of Electromagnetic Radiation to Ordnance.

Purpose: Protect people, fuel, and weapons from RF energy.

106.12 Aircraft Armament Equipment (AAE) terms​

GITR​

Gun Inventory Tracking and Reporting. Tracks gun assets.

AWIS​

All Weapons Information System. Tracks ordnance inventory and accountability.

CODR​

Conventional Ordnance Discrepancy Reporting. Reports ordnance defects or failures.

Purpose: Traceability and safety.

106.13 Responsibilities of ordnance personnel (O and I level)​

O-Level​

  • Load/download weapons
  • Inspect
  • Minor maintenance
  • Daily handling

I-Level​

  • Repair/test components
  • Deeper maintenance
  • Calibration
  • Support multiple squadrons

Both must:

  • Follow procedures
  • Maintain certifications
  • Ensure accountability
  • Enforce safety standards

Quick Oral Board Summary

If asked: "What is your responsibility for ordnance as a maintenance leader?"

Answer:

"My responsibility is ensuring ordnance operations are safe, controlled, and performed only by certified personnel using proper procedures. We maintain strict accountability and compliance because one mistake with explosives can result in catastrophic loss of life or aircraft."
